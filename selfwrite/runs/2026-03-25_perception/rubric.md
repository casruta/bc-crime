# Section 5 Perception Gap — Scoring Rubric

**Audience:** Policy-makers (cabinet ministers, municipal leaders, police chiefs)
**Tone:** Corrective — data is right, perception is wrong, close the information gap
**Depth:** Data-first, theory light

## Dimensions

### 1. Corrective Clarity (Weight: 0.30)
**Definition:** Does the text unambiguously establish that crime IS falling and that perception IS wrong, while explaining why the gap exists?
- **1-2:** Validates public perception as reasonable, doesn't clearly assert the data's authority
- **5-6:** States the data is correct but hedges or gives equal weight to perception
- **9-10:** Leads with the data being right, explains the gap as an information problem with identifiable causes, gives the policy-maker a clear framework for responding

### 2. Data Grounding (Weight: 0.25)
**Definition:** Every claim anchored in specific numbers from this analysis, not generic assertions.
- **1-2:** Vague references to "media" or "visibility" without data
- **5-6:** Some numbers present but unevenly; some mechanisms lack data backing
- **9-10:** Every mechanism cites a specific figure, rate, or comparison from the repo's own analysis

### 3. Policy Utility (Weight: 0.20)
**Definition:** Does a policy-maker know what to DO about the perception gap after reading this?
- **1-2:** Describes the gap with no actionable takeaway
- **5-6:** Implies responses but doesn't name them
- **9-10:** Each mechanism maps to a corrective action; the policy-maker can assign tasks after reading

### 4. Conciseness (Weight: 0.15)
**Definition:** Tight enough for a busy decision-maker; no redundancy or filler.
- **1-2:** Rambling, repetitive, could be cut by 50%
- **5-6:** Generally tight but some paragraphs could be compressed
- **9-10:** Every sentence earns its place; a policy-maker can read the section in under 3 minutes

### 5. Logical Flow (Weight: 0.10)
**Definition:** Arguments build on each other; reading order feels inevitable.
- **1-2:** Mechanisms feel like a random list
- **5-6:** Some logical progression but could be reordered without loss
- **9-10:** Each mechanism sets up the next; the conclusion follows inevitably from the sequence
