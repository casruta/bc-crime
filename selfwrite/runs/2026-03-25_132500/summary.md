## Key Learnings

1. **Specificity is the highest-leverage improvement.** Replacing vague quantifiers with concrete numbers produced the largest single-iteration gain (+0.50). Numbers aren't decoration — they're the substrate that makes every other dimension work.

2. **Point-first structure and audience calibration reinforce each other.** When section headings became findings (iteration 2), audience calibration improved as a bonus. Decision-makers skim headings; making headings informative serves both structure and audience.

3. **Intellectual honesty is structural, not just tonal.** Adding "What This Analysis Cannot Answer" (iteration 5) improved both Analytical Depth and Structure simultaneously. Bounding claims gives the reader a framework for trusting the claims that remain.

## Hypotheses That Worked

1. **Iteration 3 (specificity):** "Adding specific numbers where claims are vague will improve Specificity from 6 to 7." Delta: +0.50. The largest gain came from the simplest change — swapping adjectives for data.

2. **Iteration 5 (analytical depth):** "Adding named confounders and a 'cannot answer' section will improve Analytical Depth from 7 to 8." Delta: +0.40. Naming what you don't know is as analytically rigorous as stating what you do.

3. **Iteration 6 (audience calibration):** "Adding a bottom-line blockquote and why-now framing will improve Audience Calibration from 7 to 8." Delta: +0.35. The 10-second executive read and urgency framing are complementary moves.

## What Didn't Work

No iterations were reverted. All 6 produced gains. This suggests the rubric dimensions were well-chosen and the low-hanging fruit was genuine.
