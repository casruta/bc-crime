# BC Crime Findings Summary — Scoring Rubric

## Dimensions

### 1. Specificity (Weight: 0.25)
**Definition:** Concrete numbers, named entities, precise data points vs. vague generalities.
- **1-2:** Generic statements ("crime is changing"), no numbers
- **5-6:** Key statistics present but inconsistent; some claims unsupported
- **9-10:** Every claim backed by a specific number, source, or comparison; reader never wonders "how much?"

### 2. Structure (Weight: 0.20)
**Definition:** Logical flow, clear sections, point-first paragraphs, reader can navigate and skim.
- **1-2:** Wall of text, no sections, no hierarchy
- **5-6:** Sections exist but flow is uneven; some buried leads
- **9-10:** Every section opens with its conclusion; hierarchy guides the reader from headline to detail

### 3. Analytical Depth (Weight: 0.25)
**Definition:** Goes beyond description to explain causation, context, and implications.
- **1-2:** Lists facts with no interpretation
- **5-6:** Some "so what" but misses causal reasoning or alternative explanations
- **9-10:** Every finding has context (temporal, relative, causal); limitations acknowledged; competing explanations weighed

### 4. Audience Calibration (Weight: 0.15)
**Definition:** Tone and complexity match a policy-maker or informed public reader.
- **1-2:** Too technical (R code references) or too simplistic (no nuance)
- **5-6:** Mostly appropriate but occasionally drops into jargon or oversimplifies
- **9-10:** Accessible without dumbing down; technical terms explained on first use; urgency calibrated to evidence

### 5. Actionability (Weight: 0.15)
**Definition:** Reader knows what matters and what to do next; "so what" is answered.
- **1-2:** Report ends with no implications or next steps
- **5-6:** Some implications stated but generic ("more research needed")
- **9-10:** Specific, data-driven recommendations tied to findings; priorities clear
