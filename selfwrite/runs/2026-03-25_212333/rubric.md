# Scoring Rubric — BC Crime README Rewrite

**Audience**: Data analysts & researchers + General public
**Purpose**: Inform
**Register**: Level 2 (Formal analytical — Economist/OECD style)
**Mode**: Simple rewrite

## Dimensions

### 1. Factual Accuracy (Weight: 0.25)
**Definition**: All claims, numbers, and attributions are correct and verifiable.
- **1-2**: Multiple factual errors; numbers contradict source data
- **5-6**: Mostly accurate; 1-2 minor errors or imprecise claims
- **9-10**: Every number traces to source; no errors; caveats stated where appropriate

### 2. Structure & Navigation (Weight: 0.20)
**Definition**: Logical organization, scannable headings, clear section hierarchy, reader can find information quickly.
- **1-2**: Wall of text; no clear hierarchy; reader must read everything to find anything
- **5-6**: Clear sections; logical ordering; missing table of contents or methodology detail
- **9-10**: Table of contents; point-first sections; methodology complete; reproducibility instructions clear

### 3. Analytical Depth (Weight: 0.20)
**Definition**: Findings provide insight beyond description; analysis explains patterns, not just lists data.
- **1-2**: Descriptive only — "Figure X shows Y" without interpretation
- **5-6**: Some analytical framing; mix of description and insight; causal reasoning attempted
- **9-10**: Every finding answers "so what?"; patterns explained; alternative explanations addressed; limitations acknowledged

### 4. Register Discipline (Weight: 0.15)
**Definition**: Voice stays within register 2 throughout; no journalistic hooks, dramatic verbs, rhetorical questions, or scene-setting.
- **1-2**: Reads like opinion journalism; direct address, rhetorical questions, punchy one-liners
- **5-6**: Mostly formal; occasional drift to register 3-4 in captions or transitions
- **9-10**: Consistently third-person analytical; controlled observations; no editorial anti-patterns

### 5. Audience Calibration (Weight: 0.10)
**Definition**: Accessible to general public without sacrificing analytical depth for researchers.
- **1-2**: Assumes specialized knowledge; jargon unexplained; no context for non-experts
- **5-6**: Some terms explained; inconsistent depth calibration
- **9-10**: Key terms defined on first use; dual-layer structure (findings readable by all, detail available for analysts)

### 6. Evidence Integration (Weight: 0.10)
**Definition**: Charts, tables, and data are woven into analysis, not mechanically listed.
- **1-2**: "Figure X shows Y. Figure Z displays W." — mechanical listing
- **5-6**: Mix of analytical and mechanical chart references; some charts well-integrated
- **9-10**: Every chart reference leads with the finding, not the figure number; charts are evidence for claims, not standalone exhibits

**Rubric locked.**
