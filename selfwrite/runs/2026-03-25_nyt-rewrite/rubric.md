# BC Crime NYT Rewrite -- Scoring Rubric

## Dimensions

### 1. Narrative Pull (Weight: 0.25)
**Definition:** Does the piece read like journalism, not an analytical report? Opening hook, forward momentum, payoff at the end.
- **1-2:** Reads like a technical appendix; no hook, no arc, no reason to keep reading
- **5-6:** Some journalistic framing but lapses into report-speak; transitions feel mechanical
- **9-10:** Opens with a hook that creates tension; each section pulls the reader forward; the ending lands with conviction

### 2. Data Integration (Weight: 0.25)
**Definition:** Are charts introduced with context and commented on with findings? Do numbers land with impact through comparisons, ratios, and surprises?
- **1-2:** Charts dumped without context; numbers stated without framing
- **5-6:** Most charts have context but some are orphaned; numbers present but not all land with impact
- **9-10:** Every chart is introduced before it appears and commented on after; every number is framed with a comparison that makes the reader feel the scale

### 3. Audience Calibration (Weight: 0.20)
**Definition:** Would an NYT reader follow this without criminology background? Jargon explained on first use? Sentence length varied? Tone conversational but authoritative?
- **1-2:** Assumes specialist knowledge; dense paragraphs; monotone sentence rhythm
- **5-6:** Mostly accessible but occasional unexplained jargon; sentence length uniform
- **9-10:** Every technical term explained on first use; sentence rhythm varies (short punches mixed with longer compounds); feels like reading The Upshot

### 4. Specificity (Weight: 0.15)
**Definition:** Every claim backed by a specific number, named example, or direct comparison. No vague quantifiers.
- **1-2:** Vague assertions ("crime changed significantly"), no numbers
- **5-6:** Key stats present but some claims unsupported; occasional "significant" without a number
- **9-10:** Every claim has a number; reader never wonders "how much?"

### 5. Structure (Weight: 0.15)
**Definition:** Point-first paragraphs, clear sections, scannable without losing narrative thread.
- **1-2:** Wall of text; sections unlabeled; no hierarchy
- **5-6:** Sections exist but some paragraphs bury the lead; flow uneven
- **9-10:** Every section opens with its finding; hierarchy guides the reader from headline to evidence to so-what
